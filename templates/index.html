About Project Billboard

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

  • 2nd April 2019

    Beginning of Project...

    The “Project Billboard” team was formed. The task was to deliver a model predicting if a song would be a Billboard hit.

  • 18th April 2019

    Exploring possibilities...

    We had our first meeting at starbucks where we worked on the definition of “popularity”, identifying “bad songs” and rationalizing the scope of the project. Spotify’s Application Programming Interface (API) was chosen as our primary source of data.

  • 29th April 2019

    Song extraction and analysis...

    The main code for collecting songs from Spotify APIs was up and running and we had our first dataset. We proceeded to try simple algorithms for hit/miss prediction, as well as scraping Billboard Hot 100 charts for unique hits.

  • 6th May 2019

    Training of models...

    Machine learning models proved decent, with accuracies of 60-70%. The next step was to tidy up our code and prepare a user-friendly interface.

  • 11th May 2019

    Product complete...

    The project was ready for submission!

K Nearest Neighbours Model
Input: Continuous variables Output: Discrete classification Brief Description: To classify a point A, its n nearest neighbors are found by calculating the “distances” (e.g. Euclidean) it has from other points. Nearest neighbors are points with the smallest “distances” from A. The classifications of these n nearest neighbors determine the classification of A.
